# STA518 - Final Semester Project
# The Affordability Squeeze  
**How Rising Costs Have Outpaced Income Across American Counties (2009–2023)**

## Overview
This project analyzes whether income growth kept pace with inflation across U.S. counties from 2009–2023. By combining American Community Survey (ACS) data with Consumer Price Index (CPI) data, we examine trends in real income, housing costs, and poverty.

## Key Findings
- Prices rose ~42% over the study period  
- Income growth lagged in high-poverty counties  
- Housing burden (>30% income on rent) is concentrated in lower-income areas  
- The income growth gap between high- and low-poverty counties is statistically significant  

## Data Sources
- U.S. Census Bureau (ACS 5-Year Estimates)  
- Bureau of Labor Statistics (CPI-U)

## Methods
- Data cleaning and merging (county-level panel data)  
- Inflation adjustment to 2024 dollars  
- Exploratory data analysis and visualization  
- Permutation test to assess income growth differences  

## Tech Stack
R, tidyverse, ggplot2, sf, leaflet, tidymodels
